Pori City

Pori is a city and municipality on the west coast of Finland. The city is located some 10 kilometres from the Gulf of Bothnia, on the estuary of the Kokemäki River, 110 kilometres west of Tampere, 140 kilometres north of Turku and 241 kilometres north-west of Helsinki, the capital of Finland. Pori was established in 1558 by Duke John, who later became John III of Sweden.

The city has a population of 83,693 (31 December 2020) and covers an area of 2,062.00 square kilometres(796.14 sq mi) of which 870.01 km2 (335.91 sq mi) is water. The population density is 100.34/km2 (259.9/sq mi). The municipality is unilingually Finnish. It is the 10th largest city in Finland, and the 7th largest urban area. Pori is also the capital of the Satakunta region (pop. 224,028) and the Pori sub-region (pop. 136,905). Pori was also once one of the main cities with Turku in the former Turku and Pori Province (1634–1997).

Pori has a humid continental climate (Dfb), with moderation from the Gulf of Bothnia helping to keep September above the 10°C (50°F) isotherm, and is amongst the northern extent of that climate in Finland. Summers experience mild days with cool nights. Winters are long, and cold, but not extremely cold.
